How to use
Getting the most out of MenuMatch
1
Show your diet card to the server

Open the Diet card tab and hand your phone to the server, or print/copy the card text before you go. It leads with what you can eat — so staff can suggest a match instead of guessing around a list of no's — plus the one rule every dish must follow.

2
Search for restaurants near you

Go to the Find restaurants tab. You have two ways to search:

By location: Type a city name, address, or zip code into the search box and hit Search. Results will show restaurants within the selected radius.

Near me: Click the Near me button to automatically use your current GPS location — your browser will ask for permission the first time.

Use the radius dropdown (5 / 10 / 25 mi) to widen or narrow how far the search reaches. Use the cuisine filter buttons to narrow results to South Indian, Italian, Eastern European, or Japanese — the cuisines most compatible with your diet.

3
Use "Ask about these dishes" as a starting point

Each card lists dishes from your known-safe food list that might appear on that restaurant's menu. These are conversation starters for your call-ahead — not guarantees of safety. Almost everything requires kitchen confirmation.

Nothing can be assumed safe without calling first. Garlic and onion are hidden in sauces, marinades, and stocks. Rennet type requires asking the kitchen. Always call before visiting.
4
Call ahead with the script

Tap Call script on any restaurant card to get a ready-to-read phone script. It includes the restaurant's name and a clear explanation of your dietary needs so you can confirm they can accommodate you before making a trip.

5
Save places you trust

After a successful meal, tap Save on the restaurant card and add a personal note (e.g. "Ask for Maria, she knows my order"). Saved places appear in the Safe places tab. Note: saves are stored in this browser only — they won't carry over to other devices.

6
Share a restaurant with someone

Tap Share on any card to copy the restaurant's name, address, phone number, and suggested safe dishes to your clipboard. Paste it into a text or email to share with a travel companion.
